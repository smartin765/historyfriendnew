Martin Luther King: A Hero of Equality

Martin Luther King Jr. was a brave and kind person who wanted everyone to be treated fairly.

Fun Facts:

Martin Luther King Jr. was born on January 15, 1929, in Atlanta, Georgia.

He loved to play baseball and ride his bike with his friends.

He was a great speaker even when he was young, and he won a lot of speaking contests.

Martin loved reading books and learning new things.

He skipped two grades in school because he was so smart!

Timelines:

1929 Martin Luther King Jr. was born in Atlanta, Georgia.

1955 He helped lead a bus boycott to fight against unfair rules on buses.

1957 Martin helped start a group to work for civil rights and equality for all people.

1963 He gave his famous "I Have a Dream" speech during a big march in Washington, D.C.

1964 Martin Luther King Jr. won the Nobel Peace Prize for his work to make the world a better place.

1968 Sadly, he was shot and killed, but his dream of equality lives on.

Martin Luther King's Impact on the World:

He believed that everyone should be friends and live together in peace. His dream of equality and love still inspires people today to make the world a better place for everyone.
