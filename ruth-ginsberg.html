Ruth Bader Ginsburg

Ruth Bader Ginsburg's dedication to justice and equality made her a powerful role model for people of all ages. Her work will always be remembered as a shining example of how one person's actions can have a huge impact on the world.

Timelines:

1933: Ruth Bader Ginsburg is born in Brooklyn, New York.

1950s-1960s: She faces challenges as a woman studying law but doesn't give up.

1973: Ginsburg becomes a professor and fights for equal rights for women.

1993: She becomes a Supreme Court Justice, one of the highest judges in the country.

2000s-2020s: Ginsburg continues to make important decisions and inspire many.

Impact on the World:

Ruth Bader Ginsburg made history and worked for fairness and equality:

Gender Equality: She fought for women's rights and helped change laws that treated men and women unfairly.

Supreme Court: Ginsburg made decisions that protected people's rights and made sure the laws were fair.

Role Model: She showed that anyone, no matter their background, can achieve big things through hard work and dedication.

Legacy: Ginsburg inspired people to stand up for what's right and keep pushing for a fairer world.

Fun Facts:

Ruth Bader Ginsburg was born on March 15, 1933, in Brooklyn, New York.

She was known for wearing fancy collars and was sometimes called the "Notorious RBG" by her fans.

Ginsburg loved opera and even appeared in one!
